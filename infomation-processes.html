INaaS & BPaaS

Infomation as a Service (INaaS)

In the digital age, companies usually hold large data sets from SaaS software such as sales infomation from CRM systems and Web traffic data from Analytic software. Sucessful organisations try to turn this data into infomation that they can use to get a sene of what is happening within their business or institution and to predict to help them make decisions in the future.

This is where INaaS comes in, it can quickly turn this data into infomation that can be delivered to users within the organisation in real time. six examples of how INaaS could be used are:

  1. Tax Infomation
  2. Cataloging Services
  3. Curriculum Infomation
  4. Public Transport Infomation
  5. Regulatiory Infomation
  6. Medical Infomation

“Knowledge is of two kinds. We know a subject ourselves, or we know where we can find information on it.”

― Samuel Johnson

Business Process as a Service (INaaS)

The last level of abstraction in cloud computing is Business Process as a Service, this is the service that takes the infomation generated from INaaS (which in turn was taken from data generated by SaaS software) and using it to perform business processes.The following are 10 examples of how BPaaS can be used be an organisation:

  1. Taxation & Tax Planning
  2. Human Resources
  3. Payroll Functioning
  4. Exam & Testing Services
  5. Home Security
  6. Training
  7. Auditing
  8. Health Monitoring
  9. Financial Planning & Budgeting
  10. Regulatory Compitence

An example SaaS / INaaS / BPaaS pyramid wokrs is a telesales company, say the employee's are using a SaaS CRM to log sales, each sale is stored in a database as a piece of data which can then be turned into infomation such as revenue and sales per employee. An API from the Human Resources can be used to show the wages of the employees and thn proift per employee can be calculated. At the end of the year the company will need to pay taxes on profit made, this infomation could be made to perfrom the business process of compiling a tax return and even making the payment to HMRC.
